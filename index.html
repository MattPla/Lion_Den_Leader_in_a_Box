The System

Four layers. One tote.
Zero blank pages.

The Den in a Box is not a supply bin. It is an integrated support system where every layer handles a different part of what makes first-year leadership hard.

The Tote

A rolling utility tote with 13 labeled adventure bins, reusable core supplies, a program guide binder, and QR survey panels mounted inside the lid. Leaders check it out ahead of time or the mentor brings it ready. No Monday night scrambling.

The Modules

All 13 Lion program adventures in individual labeled bins. Every consumable pre-counted, pre-bagged for 20 Scouts. A laminated Quick Start Sheet and QR depletion card in each lid. Nothing to source. Nothing to improvise.

The Mentor

An experienced pack volunteer paired one-to-one with each new leader before the first meeting. Present at every meeting — not to run it, but to stand beside the leader. The box handles materials. The mentor handles confidence.

The Feedback Loop

Three QR surveys inside the tote lid. Leader survey: under 2 minutes post-cleanup. Consumable ledger: tracked during reset. Quartermaster audit: at tote return. Every meeting generates structured outcome data from day one.

The Pilot

How the pilot runs.

A phased, data-driven approach designed from day one to generate outcomes worth sharing with Council.

01
Design and planning

Finalize box format, mentor pairing protocol, and QR survey structure. Recruit 1–2 new Lion den leaders. Brief mentors. Confirm Council survey alignment.

02
Build and assemble

Source all supplies. Assemble tote and all 13 module kits. Complete Onboarding Guide and Quick Start sheet. Test tote with one dry run before pilot launch.

03
Pilot launch

First meeting. QR surveys activated. Mentor begins weekly check-ins. Leader runs first required adventure. Post-meeting debrief with mentor and coordinator.

04
Active pilot — months 3–6

Leader runs all six required adventures plus selected electives. Surveys completed after every meeting. Tote audited after every checkout. Monthly data review.

05
Evaluation and report

Compile all survey data. Mentor submits final observation report. Leader completes end-of-pilot interview. One-page summary delivered to pack and Council within 30 days.

06
Expansion recommendation

Present findings. Recommend next step: pack library, district pilot, or council-level lending model — grounded in evidence, not optimism.

The data this pilot will generate for Council.

Five questions. Six adventures. Twenty Scouts. Clean, structured outcome data collected from day one — none of it requiring Council to fund, staff, or design the collection.

The Three-Step Scale Path
Step 1
One tote. One den. Twenty Scouts. Six required adventures. Data loop active from day one.
Step 2
Pack library. Multiple dens. Shared quartermaster. Expansion built on pilot data.
Step 3
District or council model. Reservation system, deposit structure, bulk purchasing. Council owns the reusable layer.
